THE 2015 INTERNATIONAL BALLOON FIESTA

For the past 42 years, for nine days in October, the crisp, clear desert skies are have been painted with a kaleidoscope of color as hundreds of balloons list off from Albuquerque’s Balloon Fiesta Park. Nothing rivals the power of Mass ascension on crisp early mornings as these graceful giants leave the ground to take their place in the cerulean desert sky. There’s something for everyone to enjoy: whimsical special shapes filled with equal parts of hot air and wonder, and Balloon Flows that create a magical night landscape for guests to wander.

OUR HISTORY

Beginning in 1972 as the highlight of a 50th birthday celebration for 770 KOB Radio with the gathering of thirteen balloons from seven states, it was held in the parking lot of the local shopping mall with 20,000 spectators. The following year Albuquerque hosted the first World Hot-Air Balloon Championships, and the fiesta became an international event.


The International Balloon Fiesta has grown each year for decades, and today is the largest balloon festival in the world. On any given day during the festival, up to 100,000 spectators may be on the launch field where they are provided the rare opportunity to observe inflation and take off procedures. Countless more people gather at landing sites all over the city to watch incoming balloons.

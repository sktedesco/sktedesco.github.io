MESMER: The Works of Andy Gilmore

Exhibition Book Design

Created as an addition for an art exhibition, Mesmer: The Works of Andy Gilmore can also be read as a standalone piece. A collection of Gilmore's work, beginning with those of pen and pencil and leading into his digital creations, take visual precedence within.

Consisting of four sections, each of which focuses on a specific style of Gilmore's, the placement of imagery and text are done with the designs in mind. Using strong directional spacing, the design is meant to lead reader through each spread in such a way that it does not disrupt the geometry of the artwork contained.

Text placement is decided with a diagonal design, with the angle of text columns based on it's position as a recto or verso page.

Imagery is places in one of four design spreads, each working together with the text and inherent design of the work. When jumping the gitter, the primary color of the work is extended to the secondary page it shares space with. These color blocks are used to unify the elements of the spread.
